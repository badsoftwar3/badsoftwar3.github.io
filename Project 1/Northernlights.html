THE OTHERWORDLY AURORA

borwlia, or northern lights, begin high in the Earth's atmosphere--at altitudes from 60 to more than 250 miles - when charged particles from the sun become trapped in the Earth's magnetic field. The results is a colorful, dancing lightshow.

The first time I filmed an aurora was from an icebreaker near Antarctica.
